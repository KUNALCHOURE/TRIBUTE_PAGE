Tribute to Veer Savarkar


1883-1966

Introduction

Vinayak Damodar Savarkar, popularly known as Veer Savarkar, was an Indian freedom fighter, poet, writer, and social reformer. Born on May 28, 1883, in Bhagur, Maharashtra, he was a key figure in the Indian independence movement. Savarkar was known for his revolutionary activities, literary works, and his role in promoting Hindutva philosophy.

Early Life and Education

Veer Savarkar was born into a Brahmin family and displayed exceptional intelligence and a fervent patriotic spirit from a young age. He completed his primary education in his hometown and later attended Fergusson College in Pune, where he became actively involved in the struggle for independence. His commitment to the cause led him to form a youth organization called Mitra Mela, which later evolved into the revolutionary group Abhinav Bharat.

Revolutionary Activities

Savarkar moved to London in 1906 to study law, where he continued his revolutionary activities. He founded the Free India Society, aimed at achieving complete independence from British rule. In 1909, he wrote "The First War of Indian Independence," a book that provided a nationalist perspective on the 1857 revolt. His involvement in revolutionary activities led to his arrest and subsequent transportation to the Cellular Jail in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, where he endured severe hardships.

Literary Contributions

Veer Savarkar was a prolific writer and poet. His literary works played a crucial role in inspiring nationalistic feelings among Indians. Some of his notable works include: "The First War of Indian Independence": A detailed account of the 1857 revolt, providing a nationalist perspective. "Hindutva: Who is a Hindu?": A seminal work that laid the foundation of the Hindutva ideology, emphasizing the cultural and national identity of Hindus. "Kamala": A touching narrative that reflects his poetic prowess and deep emotional connect with India's cultural heritage.

Social Reforms

Veer Savarkar was a strong advocate for social reforms. He worked tirelessly to abolish caste discrimination and untouchability. He believed in a united Hindu society and promoted the idea of a casteless, harmonious community. His efforts towards social reforms were aimed at building a strong and unified society to effectively resist colonial rule and foster progress in post-independence India.

Hindutva Philosophy

Savarkar's Hindutva philosophy emphasized the cultural unity of Hindus and promoted the idea of India as a Hindu nation. His book "Hindutva: Who is a Hindu?" articulated this vision, which continues to influence socio-political discourse in India. The philosophy of Hindutva was not just religious but also a cultural and political identity that Savarkar believed was crucial for national unity and strength.

Legacy and Honors

Veer Savarkar's contributions to India's freedom struggle and social upliftment are remembered and honored through various means: Institutions and universities named after him. Statues and memorials erected in his honor. Commemorative stamps issued by the Indian government. His birth anniversary is celebrated with great reverence across the country. Conclusion

Veer Savarkar's life is a testament to unwavering patriotism, intellectual brilliance, and a relentless spirit of reform. His contributions to India's independence movement, social reforms, and the development of a nationalistic ideology continue to inspire generations. He remains a revered figure in Indian history, remembered for his dedication to the nation and his vision for a united and progressive India.
