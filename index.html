Tribute to Veer Savarkar


1883-1966

Introduction

Veer Savarkar was a prominent freedom fighter, poet, writer, and social reformer who played a crucial role in India's struggle for independence. Born on May 28, 1883, in Bhagur, a village near Nashik, he was a key figure in inspiring and mobilizing the Indian people towards the goal of complete independence from British rule. This page highlights some of his remarkable achievements and contributions. Achievements Freedom Fighter

Contribution:

Veer Savarkar was one of the first leaders to call for complete independence from British rule. His revolutionary activities, including the formation of the Free India Society and his involvement in the India House in London, inspired many others to join the freedom movement. Significance: He was sentenced to life imprisonment in the Cellular Jail in Andaman and Nicobar Islands, where he endured immense hardships but continued to inspire his fellow prisoners and the larger freedom struggle. Literary Contributions
As a prolific writer, Savarkar penned several works that highlighted the valor and sacrifice of India's historical figures. His most notable works include "The First War of Indian Independence," which provided a nationalist perspective on the 1857 revolt, and his poetry collection that instilled a sense of patriotism. Significance: His writings played a critical role in awakening nationalistic feelings among Indians and provided intellectual underpinnings to the freedom struggle. Social Reforms
Veer Savarkar was a staunch advocate of social reforms and worked tirelessly to eradicate social evils such as untouchability and caste discrimination. He promoted the idea of a casteless and united Hindu society. His efforts towards social reforms were aimed at uniting the Indian society to present a strong front against colonial rule and to build a harmonious and progressive nation post-independence. Hindutva Philosophy
Savarkar was the proponent of the Hindutva ideology, which he articulated in his seminal work "Hindutva: Who is a Hindu?" He envisioned Hindutva as a cultural and national identity that encompassed the ethos and values of the Indian civilization. His Hindutva philosophy has continued to influence socio-political discourse in India and remains a significant part of his legacy. Legacy and Honors
Veer Savarkar's legacy is remembered and honored through various means such as institutions named after him, statues, and commemorative stamps. His life and works continue to inspire millions of Indians. His birth anniversary is celebrated with great reverence, and his contributions to India's independence and social reforms are remembered with pride. Conclusion Veer Savarkar's life is a testament to unwavering patriotism, intellectual prowess, and a relentless spirit of reform. His contributions to India's freedom struggle and social upliftment continue to resonate, making him a revered figure in Indian history.
